About Amy

Ling Ma’s Severance explores the constant battle between outside motivators and internal desires to chronicle the character evolution of Candace Chen. Her unsteady boyfriend, Jonathan, serves as a foil to explore the impact of an unbalanced work-life dynamic. Jonathan and Chen’s relationship presents the protagonist with another set of difficulties that force her to face the inescapable global health crisis. In the post-pandemic timeline, Bob becomes her newest and greatest challenge through a characterization applicable to contemporary political figures. While it seems the protagonist overcomes and continues to rise despite the problems she faces, at the end of the piece, Chen’s situation is even more precarious than at the beginning of the novel. Candance Chen’s experiences with aggressive male figures and an unplanned pregnancy reveal the significance of considering the well-being of others and standing against abuses of power to work towards personal growth.
